One website, one domain

Why consolidating your public web presence makes sense

Prepared for Chiropractic Associates & The Rub Club — Paris & Sulphur Springs, TX

In one sentence

We are not removing your online presence — we are organizing it. Patients, Google, and your staff get one primary website with clear sections for each location and each service (massage and chiropractic). Your existing web addresses can keep working by automatically forwarding visitors to the right place on the new site.

The situation today

You currently have multiple public websites (for example, separate sites for massage-focused marketing, Paris chiropractic, and Sulphur Springs). That made sense in the past, but it creates ongoing duplication:

  • Hours, insurance, providers, services, forms, and promos must be updated in more than one place — or the sites slowly disagree with each other and with your Google Business Profile.
  • Patients and referral sources can land on different “official” websites with different navigation and messaging.
  • Marketing links (Google Ads, email signatures, print materials) get split across domains, so nobody is sure which link is canonical.

https://www.chiropracticparistexas.com/
https://www.massageparistexas.com/
https://www.chiropracticsulphursprings.com/

The recommendation

One primary domain. One modern website. Inside that site, you still have everything you need:

  • Distinct location pages (Paris vs Sulphur Springs).
  • Distinct service sections (massage vs chiropractic vs injury care, etc.).
  • Online booking and contact paths that match how you actually operate.

Your older domains remain valuable as entry points: they can permanently forward to the matching page on the main site so bookmarks and old ads still work.

What this fixes (without “SEO homework”)

This approach is not about chasing trends. It is about reducing maintenance and confusion:

Area Multiple sites One site, one domain
Updates Change hours/services in several places Update once in the admin panel; the public site stays consistent
Patient experience “Which site is real?” One front door; clear paths to each office and service
Google Business Profile Easy to mismatch website URL and on-site info One website URL to maintain across listings
Ads & email Pick which domain to promote One domain on cards, ads, and signatures

A solid technical baseline (fast pages, clear titles, sitemap, accurate business information) can be built into the new site up front. The part that still needs occasional attention is real-world accuracy — providers, services, hours — which you can manage from the superadmin panel without ongoing “SEO research.”

What we are not claiming: No one can guarantee Google rankings or a specific map radius. One domain does not mean one Google listing if you have two real addresses — you should still maintain appropriate Google Business Profiles per location (Paris vs Sulphur Springs), all pointing to the same primary website.

Launch sequence (simple)

  1. New site goes live on the primary (chiropractic) domain.
  2. Old sites forward to the new site so important old links open the closest matching new page when possible.
  3. Google Business Profile “Website” fields are updated to the primary domain.
  4. Google Ads (if used) are updated so ad URLs match where patients land.
  5. You edit content going forward through the superadmin panel — not by logging into three separate website builders.

Common questions

“Don’t we need separate sites so massage shows up in Google separately?”
No. Search engines rank pages, not “number of domains.” You can have strong massage pages and strong chiropractic pages on the same website. Separate sites often split reviews, links, and consistent branding without adding a patient benefit.

“Will we lose patients who only know the old web address?”
No, if redirects are set up correctly. The old address should open the new site automatically.

“Is this only for SEO?”
It helps clarity and consistency, which supports search and ads, but the main win is operational: one place to manage the public face of the practice.

Summary

One domain and one website gives patients a clear, modern front door, reduces duplicate work for staff, aligns your Google Business Profile links with reality, and lets you manage content centrally through the superadmin panel — while preserving old web addresses via forwards so nothing feels “turned off.”

This handout describes a standard web consolidation approach. Search rankings depend on many factors outside any single project; the goal here is a maintainable, patient-friendly foundation.
